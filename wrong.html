Fashion Class Gets Cancelled

If the Fashion Promotion and Exhibition class gets cancelled or if I am unable to take the class, I will instead replace this class with another fashion-related class so I can still receive some education about the fashion industry while still in school. Some other classes that I could potentially take include: Fashion Illustration: Visualizing Apparel, Textile Design, and Topics in Fashion Design: Experimental Fashion Design.

Rejected from MBA Program

If I do not get into the Fashion & Luxury MBA Program at NYU, there are a handful of other schools I could apply to in order to earn a business degree with a focus on fashion and/or luxury management. Some other schools I could apply to are: Institut Français de la Mode (Management), Fashion Institute of Technology (Global Fashion Management, Business Management and Entrepreneurship), Parsons School of Design (Fashion Management), Central Saint Martins (Fashion—Menswear, Fashion Communication), or the International University of Monaco (Luxury Management).

Online Startup Doesn't Take Off

If my online fashion startup does not take off during the first 2-3 years after graduation, I think it would be important first to explore and understand both why the company didn’t take off, and how things could have been done differently. Regardless of the success of the startup, it will be an important learning opportunity about the fashion space and will be a valuable experience that could be placed on my resume. Some actions I could take if my streetwear startup brand is not successful include: changing my marketing approach and platforms, changing the aesthetic of my products, changing the branding, waiting until after I have received formal education in fashion management and made other connections in the fashion industry, and waiting until I have worked for several years so that I could hire someone to handle the marketing, branding, or design aspects of my business.
